# Chapter 2 - The Model

"Would you like to see the Time Machine itself?" asked the Time Traveller.

And therewith, taking the lamp in his hand, he led the way down the long, draughty corridor to his laboratory. I remember vividly the flickering light, his queer, broad head in silhouette, the dance of the shadows, how we all followed him, puzzled but incredulous, and how there in the laboratory we beheld a larger edition of the little mechanism which we had seen vanish from before our eyes.

Parts were of nickel, parts of ivory, parts had been filed or sawn out of rock crystal. The thing was generally complete, but the twisted crystalline bars lay unfinished upon the bench beside some sheets of drawings, and I took one up for a better look. Quartz it seemed to be.

"Look here," said the Medical Man, "are you really in earnest about this? Do you seriously believe that that machine has travelled into time?"

"Absolutely," said the Time Traveller, standing before the hearth. "Furthermore, I propose to use this machine myself, and to explore time. Is that plain? I have never been more in earnest in my life."

As he spoke, he looked directly at us, his face pale and serious.

None of us quite knew how to take it. I caught Filby's eye over the Psychologist's shoulder, and he winked at me solemnly.

"It is a beautiful piece of work," said the Medical Man, examining the levers and dials. "But what is the theory? How does it operate?"

"I will show you," said the Time Traveller, and he began to explain the mechanics. "The principal of the machine is based on the synchronization of the Fourth Dimension with physical rotation. When this lever is pressed forward, the machine slides into the future. When it is pulled backward, it glides into the past. These dials register the day, the year, the century, and the millennium. It is as simple as steering a bicycle, once you master the balance."

We looked at the strange structure with its gleaming brass and polished crystal. It seemed too delicate, too artistic, to be a vehicle for such a colossal journey. And yet, there was something in the Time Traveller's quiet confidence that silenced our doubts and left us wondering what mysteries of the future lay waiting to be discovered.
